Mental health & Mental disorders

Why do we still need to pay more attention to mental health issues?

Suicide is the second cause of death among people from 10 to 34, while 46% of people who committed suicide had one or more diagnosed mental health illness, according to the newest report from National Alliance on Mental Illness. In 2018, only about 40% of U.S. adults with mental illnesses received treatment. Around 60% of counties do not have single practicing psychiatrist.

Therefore, even though the society has already paid more attention to mental health issues, it is still not enough. No matter you have mental illnesses or not, we should know more about mental health issues. In this way, people can understand that having a mental illness is not a stigma, or people with mental illnesses are no different from others. On this website, the history of mental health illnesses will bu introduced. You can also test your knowledge about mental health issues on the Quiz page.

Suicide rate trend in Missouri

From this chart, it is apparent that the suicide rates underwent an increase trend in both the U.S. and Missouri from 2006 to 2018. The suicide rates in Missouri were higher than the rates in the U.S. According to American Foundation for Suicide Prevention, one person dies by committing suicide every eight hours on average in Missouri. Suicide cost Missouri $1,016,985,000 in total. The number of deaths by suicide is around 1,150, which ranked 18 among all states in the U.S.

As mentioned above, a large number of people who committed suicide had mental disorders. Therefore, as a Missourian resident, it is high time that we paid more attention to mental disorders and knew more knowledge related to this topic.If you find someone who wants to suicide, please call Access Crisis Intervention Hotline and The National Suicide Prevention Lifeline at 1-800-273-8255 to get them hospitalized ASAP. Or you can call 911 for emergency.
