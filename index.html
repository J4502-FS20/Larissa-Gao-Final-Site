Mental health & Mental disorders

Why do we still need to pay more attention to mental health issues?

Suicide is the second cause of death among people from 10 to 34, while 46% of people who committed suicide had one or more diagnosed mental health illnesses, according to the newest report from National Alliance on Mental Illness. In 2018, only about 40% of U.S. adults with mental illnesses received treatment. Around 60% of counties do not have a single practicing psychiatrist.

The chart shows the "prevalence of any mental illness among U.S. adults" in 2019, according to the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration. Hence, there are many people who have mental health issues. But the attention we pay to the topic is far from enough. The website is for people to better understand mental health issues, including statistics and history. If you want to test your knowledge about mental health issues, please visit the Quiz page.

Suicide rate trend in Missouri

From this chart, it is apparent that the suicide rates underwent an increasing trend in both the U.S. and Missouri from 2006 to 2018. The suicide rates in Missouri were higher than the rates in the U.S. According to the American Foundation for Suicide Prevention, one person dies by committing suicide every eight hours on average in Missouri. Suicide cost Missouri $1,016,985,000 in total. The number of deaths by suicide is around 1,150 in 2019, which ranked 18 among all states in the U.S. In missouri, suicide is the 10th leading cause of death. Here is the rank of suicide among the causes of death in different age groups.

As mentioned above, a large number of people who committed suicide had mental disorders. So, as a Missourian resident, it is high time that we paid more attention to mental disorders and knew more knowledge related to this topic. If you find someone who wants to suicide, please call Access Crisis Intervention Hotline, and The National Suicide Prevention Lifeline at 1-800-273-8255 to get them hospitalized ASAP. Or, you can call 911 for an emergency. If you are people with mental disorders, but you don't know the treatment for mental disorders, please check the Treatment page to get to know more about current ways to treat mental illnesses.
